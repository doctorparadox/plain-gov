# Congressional Committees Full Tree

The **expanded** ASCII-art map of the **119th Congress** (Republican majority in both chambers). This version adds every **subcommittee** and the four **joint committees** on top of the full-committee leadership. For the cleaner top-level-only version, see [[Congressional Committees Tree]].

Because Republicans hold both chambers, every Chair is a Republican and every Ranking Member is a Democrat (or an Independent caucusing with the Democrats, marked `I`).

> [!note] How to read this tree
> **Full-committee header lines** carry `C:` (Chair, R), `RM:` (Ranking Member, D), and `VC:` (Vice Chair) where one exists.
> **Subcommittee lines** use the compact form `Subcommittee name — Chair (R-ST) / Ranking Member (D-ST)` — first name is always the Republican chair, second is the Democratic ranking member.
> On Senate **Appropriations** and Senate **Intelligence**, the *minority* lead is titled "Vice Chair" instead of "Ranking Member."

> [!warning] Subcommittee data is the volatile layer
> Subcommittee gavels shift mid-Congress more often than full-committee ones. Slots that could not be confirmed to a current primary source are marked `[unconfirmed]`. The House Intelligence Committee (HPSCI) does not publicly publish a per-subcommittee leadership roster, so only its subcommittee *names* (and the new OSINT chair) are shown.

## Notes and caveats

**Subcommittee names shift under new majorities.** Several panels were renamed or reorganized for the 119th Congress. Notable examples: House Financial Services' digital-assets panel is now "Digital Assets, Financial Technology, and Artificial Intelligence" (dropping "Inclusion"); House Appropriations' State-Foreign Operations panel is now "National Security, Department of State, and Related Programs"; Senate HELP's "Children and Families" is now "Education and the American Family"; and Senate HSGAC restructured its subcommittees entirely.

**Slots flagged `[unconfirmed]`.** The House Budget "Budget Process Reform" task force lead, the House Administration Elections subcommittee ranking member, and the House Oversight "Federal Law Enforcement" ranking member (a recent, undated swap toward Greg Casar) could not be pinned to a current primary source. The House Intelligence Committee does not publish a per-subcommittee leadership roster, so only its subcommittee names and the newly created OSINT subcommittee chair (Ann Wagner) are shown.

**Mid-Congress successions already reflected.** House Homeland Security (Garbarino replaced Green after his July 2025 resignation), House Natural Resources RM (Huffman after Grijalva's death), House Oversight RM (Garcia after Connolly's death), and the House DOGE subcommittee (Burchett replaced Marjorie Taylor Greene in January 2026).

**One label quirk.** House Ways & Means' sixth subcommittee is called "Work and Welfare" by the majority and "Worker and Family Support" by the minority — same panel, two names (LaHood / Davis).
