THE APPROPRIATIONS PIPELINE — AUTHORIZATION TO OUTLAY  (annual cycle, FY starts Oct 1)
│   Legend:  ──▶ main path   └─▶ branch   ◇ decision   ✕ funding stops   ★ money spent
│
■ 0. AUTHORIZATION  (the permission — NOT the money)
│     Authorizing committees (Armed Services, Judiciary, Energy & Commerce, etc.)
│     create/continue a program and set an authorized ceiling ("such sums as...").
│     ◇ authorization lapsed? ──▶ program can still be funded (unauthorized
│        appropriations are common) — the ceiling is a guideline, not a gate.
│     └─▶ authorization ≠ dollars: a fully authorized program can receive $0.
│
├─◆ 1. THE PRESIDENT'S REQUEST  (the opening bid)
│     First Monday in February: the [[President's Budget Request]] lands, built by OMB.
│     Congress is free to ignore it — it sets the terms of debate, not the numbers.
│
├─◆ 2. THE BUDGET RESOLUTION  (Congress's top-line — no President's signature)
│     Concurrent resolution → sets total discretionary ceiling (the 302(a) allocation)
│     ├─ Appropriations Committees carve that total into 302(b) suballocations,
│     │    one per subcommittee — the real spending caps for the year
│     └─ ◇ no budget resolution passed? ──▶ deeming resolution / prior caps fill in
│          (Congress often skips the resolution entirely and appropriates anyway)
│
├─◆ 3. THE TWELVE BILLS  (the appropriations machine — House originates)
│     12 subcommittees, 12 bills, ~27% of all federal outlays (discretionary only):
│     Defense · Labor-HHS-Ed · Commerce-Justice-Science · Energy-Water · Interior-Env ·
│     Agriculture · Transportation-HUD · Financial Services · Homeland Security ·
│     MilCon-VA · State-Foreign Ops · Legislative Branch
│     ├─ subcommittee markup ──▶ full committee ──▶ floor ──▶ pass one chamber
│     ├─ repeat in the other chamber ──▶ resolve differences (conference / ping-pong)
│     └─ ◇ enacted before Oct 1? ── this is where the wheels come off (see branch ↓)
│
├─◆ 4. ENACTMENT  (presentment — Art. I, § 7)
│     President signs (or veto/override) ──▶ ★ BUDGET AUTHORITY (BA) becomes law.
│     BA = legal permission to obligate funds. Still not a dollar spent — the money
│     now has to make it through OMB and Treasury.
│
├─◆ 5. APPORTIONMENT  (OMB's release valve — the slow-walk lever)
│     OMB apportions the enacted BA back to each agency by time period, program,
│     or activity — the point at which enacted money can be quietly delayed.
│     ├─ ◇ apportionment withheld / footnoted? ──▶ funds sit unobligated
│     │      └─▶ if it becomes a policy refusal to spend ──▶ IMPOUNDMENT territory:
│     │          the [[Impoundment Control Act]] requires reporting a rescission or
│     │          deferral to Congress — see [[Rescission and Impoundment]]
│     └─ apportioned ──▶ agency may now obligate
│
├─◆ 6. OBLIGATION  (the agency commits — the legal hook)
│     Agency signs contracts, awards grants, hires — legally binding the government.
│     ◇ obligating without/beyond an appropriation? ──▶ ✕ violates the
│        [[Antideficiency Act]] (the wall that makes a shutdown a shutdown)
│
└─◆ 7. OUTLAY  (money actually leaves the Treasury)
      Treasury issues the warrant and disburses cash against the obligation.
      ★ OUTLAY — the only figure that measures what was truly spent.
      └─▶ authorized → appropriated → apportioned → obligated → OUTLAID:
          money can be lost at every arrow. The distance between the top and the
          bottom of this chain is exactly what [[The Spending Gap]] tracks.

═══ THE BRANCH THAT ACTUALLY RUNS THE GOVERNMENT ═══════════════════════════════
◇ all twelve bills enacted by Oct 1?  (on-time passage is essentially extinct)
│
├─ NO, deadline looming ──▶ CONTINUING RESOLUTION ([[Continuing Resolution]])
│     stopgap that funds agencies at (roughly) prior-year rates for days–months;
│     freezes new starts, holds programs flat, buys time. Serial CRs are the norm.
│     └─▶ at a later deadline, bills get bundled into an OMNIBUS / minibus and passed.
│
└─ NO, and the CR lapses with no deal ──▶ ✕ GOVERNMENT SHUTDOWN
      ([[Government Shutdown]]) — with no appropriation, the [[Antideficiency Act]]
      bars agencies from obligating funds; non-excepted work stops, excepted
      (safety/security) staff work unpaid until funding is restored.
      [FY2026 set the record: a 43-day shutdown, then a partial one, before all
       twelve bills were finally enacted.]

  Note: BUDGET RECONCILIATION is a DIFFERENT track — it moves mandatory spending
  and tax law by 51 votes and does NOT fund the discretionary agencies. It is not
  part of this pipeline.
