HOW A CASE REACHES THE SUPREME COURT — THE CERTIORARI FUNNEL  (per October Term)
│   Legend:  ──▶ main path   └─▶ branch/bypass   ◇ decision   ✕ case stops   ★ merits ruling
│   Scale:  ~7,000–8,000 petitions filed  →  ~60–70 argued  (grant rate ≈ 1%)
│
■ 0. A FINAL JUDGMENT BELOW  (you cannot appeal a case that isn't over)
│     A dispute is tried and loses in a U.S. District Court, then loses again on
│     appeal in a U.S. Court of Appeals (or a state's highest court on a federal
│     question). SCOTUS reviews FINAL judgments — not live, mid-trial rulings.
│     ◇ state case? ──▶ only reviewable if it turns on FEDERAL law, from the
│        state's court of last resort (the "adequate & independent state ground"
│        bar keeps purely state-law questions out).
│
├─◆ 1. HOW A CASE CAN ARRIVE  (three doors — one is used almost always)
│     ├─ CERTIORARI (discretionary) ──▶ ~99% of the docket; the rest of this map
│     ├─ ORIGINAL JURISDICTION ──▶ state-v-state & similar: filed DIRECTLY at
│     │    SCOTUS, skips the whole funnel (a handful of cases, ever)
│     └─ CERTIFIED QUESTION ──▶ a Court of Appeals asks SCOTUS a legal question
│          directly (nearly extinct in modern practice)
│
├─◆ 2. THE PETITION  (the loser asks to be heard)
│     Petitioner files a "petition for a writ of certiorari" within 90 days of the
│     final judgment. Its heart is the QUESTION PRESENTED — one clean legal issue.
│     ├─ paid docket ──▶ printed briefs, filing fee
│     └─ in forma pauperis ──▶ fee waived (prisoners, indigent parties); the bulk
│          of raw filings, a vanishing share of grants
│     Respondent files a brief in opposition; amici may file at the cert stage.
│
├─◆ 3. SCREENING  (the cert pool does the first cut)
│     Most chambers pool their law clerks; one clerk writes a single memo per
│     petition recommending grant or deny, shared across the pooled chambers.
│     ├─ flagged as certworthy ──▶ goes on the "discuss list"
│     └─ ◇ not flagged? ──▶ the "dead list" ──▶ ✕ denied without discussion
│          (the fate of the overwhelming majority of petitions)
│
├─◆ 4. THE CONFERENCE  (the nine meet in private)
│     Petitions on the discuss list are voted, most-senior justice first.
│     ◇◇◇ THE RULE OF FOUR ──▶ do 4 of the 9 vote to grant?
│     ├─ FEWER THAN 4 ──▶ ✕ CERT DENIED
│     │     The lower court's judgment STANDS — but a denial sets NO precedent
│     │     and signals nothing about the merits. (Occasionally a justice files a
│     │     "dissent from denial" to flag an issue for a future petition.)
│     └─ 4 OR MORE ──▶ ★ CERT GRANTED ──▶ continue to merits (stage 5)
│
├─◆ 5. MERITS BRIEFING & ARGUMENT  (now the case is really heard)
│     Merits briefs (petitioner, respondent) ──▶ amicus curiae briefs flood in
│     ──▶ ORAL ARGUMENT (~1 hour, questions from the bench) ──▶ the justices
│     meet in conference and take a tentative vote.
│     └─ the Solicitor General ([[Solicitor General]]) argues for the United
│        States, and the Court often invites its views (CVSG) even in private
│        disputes — the "Tenth Justice" effect.
│
└─◆ 6. THE DECISION  (opinion + judgment)
      Senior justice in the majority assigns the opinion (the Chief, if in the
      majority, always assigns). Drafts circulate; concurrences and dissents attach.
      ★ OPINION ISSUED ──▶ affirm · reverse · or vacate & remand.
      └─▶ the holding becomes binding NATIONAL precedent under [[Stare Decisis]] —
          the payoff that makes a single granted case reshape the law everywhere.

  ── Off-ramps that skip the clean 4→5→6 path ─────────────────────────────────
  • GVR (Grant, Vacate, Remand): granted, then bounced back to the lower court in
    light of an intervening ruling — no full argument.
  • DIG (Dismissed as Improvidently Granted): the Court grants, hears argument,
    then decides it should not have taken the case ──▶ cert dismissed, no merits.

═══ THE EMERGENCY TRACK — THE SHADOW DOCKET  ([[Shadow Docket]]) ════════════════
◇ a party needs the Court to act NOW — before the normal appeal can finish?
│   (a stay of execution, a stay/injunction of a policy or lower-court order)
│
├─ APPLICATION filed with the CIRCUIT JUSTICE for that circuit ──▶ referred to
│    the full Court ──▶ ruled on in days or weeks, often with NO oral argument,
│    thin or no briefing, and frequently an UNSIGNED order with no reasoning.
│
├─ ◇ 5 votes to grant relief? ──▶ ★ the lower-court order is STAYED / an
│    injunction issues — the challenged policy runs (or is blocked) immediately,
│    nationwide, while the slow merits litigation continues below.
│
└─ NOTE: this track bypasses the Rule of Four (it takes 5, not 4) and the entire
     cert-and-argument process. Increasingly the Court makes consequential,
     precedent-shaping law here — fast, unexplained, and hard to track. This is
     why the emergency docket increasingly matters as much as the merits docket.
